Income Tax Law Syllabus

Unit-I: Basic Concepts

Introduction of Income Tax law

Income tax is a direct tax levied by the government on an individual's or entity's income. The taxation system in India is governed by the Income Tax Act, 1961. The Act provides a framework for tax calculation, assessment, and collection.

Previous Year

As per Section 3 of the Income Tax Act, "Previous Year" is the financial year immediately preceding the assessment year in which income is earned. Example: If income is earned between 1st April 2023 – 31st March 2024, the previous year will be 2023-24.

Assessment Year

The year in which the income of the previous year is assessed and taxed. Example: For the previous year 2023-24, the assessment year will be 2024-25.

Assessee

An assessee is a person liable to pay tax or any other amount under the Income Tax Act. It includes individuals, firms, companies, and other entities.

Person

A person includes: An individual Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) Company Firm Association of Persons (AOP) or Body of Individuals (BOI) Local Authority Any artificial juridical person

Gross Total Income

The sum of an individual’s income from all heads before deductions under Chapter VI-A (Section 80C to 80U). Formula: GTI = Income from Salaries + Income from House Property + Profits & Gains of Business or Profession + Capital Gains + Income from Other Sources

Agricultural Income

Defined under Section 2(1A), agricultural income includes: Rent or revenue from land used for agricultural purposes. Income derived from agricultural operations. Sale of agricultural produce. Agricultural income is exempt from tax under Section 10(1), but it is considered for determining the tax rate (if non-agricultural income exists).

Income

Income includes both monetary and non-monetary gains, covering: Salary, rent, profits, dividends, interest, etc. Illegal income is also taxable.

Basis of Charge and Rates of Tax

Basis of Charge (Section 4) Tax is charged on the total income of an assessee during the previous year in the relevant assessment year. Income tax applies to all persons as per their residential status and total income. Rates of Tax The tax rate depends on the type of assessee (individual, company, firm, etc.). The Income Tax Slabs for individuals (as per the latest finance act) include Old Tax Regime & New Tax Regime.
