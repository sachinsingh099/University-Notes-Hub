ðŸ“Œ Working Capital: Meaning, Components, and Methods of Estimation

Working Capital refers to the funds required for day-to-day business operations and is calculated as Current Assets minus Current Liabilities. Efficient working capital management is essential to ensure smooth business operations, prevent liquidity crises, and enhance profitability. The major components of working capital include cash, accounts receivable, inventory, and short-term liabilities. The requirement for working capital depends on factors such as business size, industry type, credit policies, and market demand. The methods of estimating working capital include the Operating Cycle Method, which determines the time taken to convert raw materials into cash; the Projected Balance Sheet Method, which estimates future working capital needs based on projected assets and liabilities; and the Cash Forecasting Method, which calculates expected cash inflows and outflows over a period.
